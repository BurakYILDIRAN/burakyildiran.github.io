Hybrid Composite Gear

The aim of this study is to produce composite gears that approach or better the characteristic curve of the steel gear.

In this study, the aim is to determine whether the characteristic curve of the steel gear is approaching or better it is to produce composite gear with characteristic characteristics. In this way, the design is lightened without moving away from the force/deformation ratio of the gear. For this, first of all, the choice of material should be made. Analysis was carried out in a computer environment and material selection was made using these analyzes. As a result of static, frequency and buckling analysis experiments of hybrid gears, the characteristics of the materials were obtained and compared and evaluated accordingly. The gear wheel design was inspired by a hybrid gear wheel design made at the Nasa Glenn Research Center in 2012. The material assignment has been changed by keeping the gear design fixed. Then, geometry optimization was made with the selected material.
